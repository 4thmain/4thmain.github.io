India Privacy Map

Andhra Pradesh

UID: In 2010 the government of  Andhra Pradesh entered into an MOU  with the UIDAI to begin enrollment and implementation of the UID project across the State. As of April 2013, 53  districts  are carrying out UID enrollments. The nodal department  for UID is the Department of Planning In December 2012 it was found that  2.3 lakh fake  UID cards were issued by enrollment agencies in the State. The cards were issued using the biometric exemption, and have now been canceled.

NPR: In 2010 plans for  implementation of the NPR  project were discussed for the state of Andhra Pradesh, but as of 2012,  enrollment for the NPR  has been put on hold.

CCTV: In the state of Andhra Pradesh  police have used CCTV footage  in efforts to solve crimes and for law enforcement. CCTVs are also used to monitor traffic and are used by private companies. Companies that source CCTV security systems to districts in Andhra Pradesh include:  Mantra ,  Indosoft Corporation  and Spy Action India Home Products. CCTV cameras at  Secunderabad railway station reportedly  helped the police solve the theft of a handbag forgotten by a passenger.  

UAVs: In 2010 it was reported  that the Tirupati temple in Andhra Pradesh would get unmanned aerial surveillance as part of security enhancement plans for the temple, which is visited by thousands of devotees daily.

A senior official of Special Intelligence Branch (SIB), an elite anti-Maoist wing, reported  in 2012 that a UAV was used on an experimental basis in Andhra Pradesh and neighbouring Chhattisgarh and the results were positive enough for the drone to be introduced in anti-Maoist operations.

CCTNS: Andhra Pradesh is considered to be the  first state  in India to launch the CCTNS project.  In January 2012 the Tata Consultancy services (TCS) signed an MoU with the Andhra Pradesh police to implement a  crime tracking system  across the country. The tender fee for the implementation of the CCTNS is Rs. 25,000  and the Andhra Pradesh Police Department has invited sealed bids from reputed IT Companies / firms for the selection of a System Integrator (SI) for implementing and maintaining the CCTNS project across the state. The CCTNS pilot project launched in the three units  --Cyberabad, West Godavari and Chittoor in Andhra Pradesh, the DGP said the remaining districts and units in the state will be completed in second phase by September 2013. Talking to reporters after launching the project, the DGP said it is nationwide project connected to MHA,NCRB, New Delhi and all State capitals and Union Territories of the country and is a significant landmark in making police more accessible transparent and citizen friendly. According to West Godavari district Superintendent of Police M. Ramesh, the CCTNS project came into existence with three initial features —Andhra Pradesh Police Messaging System (APPMS), an investigator tool to track the identity of mobile phone and vehicle users and the Andhra Pradesh Police Website. If fully operational, the CCTNS project is expected to extend all sorts of citizen services relating to the policing to the people online, besides helping the police receive an interface with citizens and different government departments such as Prisons, RTA, Passport, Civil Supplies, Forensic Science Laboratory (FSL) and Election Commission and also receive feed from informants.

Arunachal Pradesh

UID: In June 2010, the government of Arunachal Pradesh entered into an MOU  for implementation of the UID project across the state through enrolment agencies . The UID project which aims to remove the problem of wrong identity is intended to benefit 13 lakh  people in the state. The nodal department for UID in Arunachal pradesh is the Commissioner, Economics and Statistics

NPR:In March 2010, the order to collect data for the NPR along with the population census 2011 was made. The second phase  of the census was conducted in February 2011. As per the provisional figures , the total population of the state stood at 13 Lakhs (1.3 Million). As of 2012, the government of Arunachal Pradesh invited proposals  for setting up data entry centers, bulk data entry, biometric data collection and printing of local register of usual registers in pursuance of of the NPR project.

CCTV: In July 2012, the government of Arunachal Pradesh decided to install CCTV cameras in all media establishments. This reason behind this move was apparently to instill confidence  in working journalists. Companies like Senstar Corp and SpyActionIndia  have been found to provide CCTV products in the state.

UAVs: As of November 2012, The Indian Army had reported atleast 37 sightings  of supposed Chinese drones in the easter sector of Arunachal Pradesh.

CCTNS: In 2011, the Arunachal Pradesh started preparations for implementation of the CCTNS. It invited proposals  for selection of system integrators  for commissioning and maintenance of CCTNS. In 2012, a committee was established to discuss the progress  of implementation. In February 2012, the testing of the core application software was conducted and regional workshops on network connectivity were organised.

Assam

UID: In July 2010, the government of Assam entered into an MOU  with the UIDAI for implementation of the UID project through enrollment agencies . The nodal department for the state is the Home and Political Department However the implementation of the program has seen several setbacks , the most visible being the issue of Bangladeshi migrants. In October 2012, it was decided that the AADHAR database for Assam would be linked to the National Register of Citizens. Consequently, the AADHAR number would only act as a proof of residence and not citizenship .

NPR: The NPR is being digitalized in Assam through collection of fingerprints and iris scanning. In addition, the AADHAR database will also be merged National Register of Citizens with the state government setting up an NRC directorate to undertake the excercise.

CCTV: Companies that source CCTV security systems to districts of Assam include: Spy Action India , Altronix and others.

Drones: In March 2013, it was reported that the authorities at the Kaziranga National Park plan to deploy drones to monitor poachers  by air in an effort to protect the endangered rhinos and combat poaching.

CCTNS: In 2011, the Assam started preparations for implementation of the CCTNS. It invited proposals for selection of system integrators  for commissioning and maintenance of CCTNS. The state police plans to set up 33 training labs  across the state under the scheme and the system will cover 312 police stations  across the state. The CCTNS will also be used aid the authorities in counter-insurgency operations against militant outfits .

Bihar

UID: In August 2010, the government of Bihar entered into an MoU  with the UIDAI for implementation of the UID project through enrollment agencies. The program was launched in the state capital , Patna in June 2011. The nodal department for UID in the state is the Planning and Development Department However, civil liberty groups in the state have also expressed concerns  regarding the effect of the project on the issues like privacy and have demanded to put a stay on the execution until these concerns have been addressed.

NPR: The NPR project has been introduced  in Bihar in August 2012. The NPR will implemented through collection of fingerprints and iris scanning and a pilot project has been undertaken SmartID . The NPR project scheme will also be utilised to collect data and issue Aadhar numbers to those not covered by the UID project.

CCTV: In Bihar, there are several CCTV distributors and suppliers including Life Line Safety Services , T elecom Electronics , New India Enterprises and Bajrand Marketing Co .

CCNTS: In 2010, the Bihar government started preparations  for setting up the CCTNS in the state. The system will link  all the police stations in the state with the state police headquarters and the National Crime Record Bureau in Delhi.

Chhattisgarh

UID: The government of Chattisgarh entered into an MoU  with the UIDAI in April 2010 for the implementation of the UID project through enrollment agencies. The nodal department for the UID project in the state is the Department of FInance and Planning. The first phase  of the project covers 3 districts in the state cover around 50 lakh (5 million) citizens. The state government intends to integrate the public distribution system  and other public welfare systems to the project. However actual implementation of the project has remained uncertain  mainly due to the opposition posed by Maoist guerrillas.

NPR: As per the 2011 census, the total population of Chattisgarh stands at 2,08,33,803 . The NPR is also being digitized and records of around 1 crore people have been digitized. However, reports have indicated that census exercise has not been undertaken in several hundred villages  mainly due to the presence of naxals and them being located in heavily forested areas.

CCTV: There are several CCTV equipment dealers and distributors which include Ultrasafe Equipments Pvt. Ltd , SN Agarwal and Co and Radha Marketing .

Drones: Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAV) are being used extensively in Chhattisgarh which is the worst affected by Maoist violence, by the Police and CRPF  in their efforts to hunt and track Maoists mainly in the heavily forested regions of state. As per reports UAV’s are used to gather information on location of camps , movement of insurgents and detect mines and IEDs. The Drones which have operated from Hyderabad are being shifted to Bhilai  in Chhattisgarh. In April 2013, a UAV was used to record an encounter between the security forces and Maoists. However, UAV’s have not been entirely successful   in providing sufficient intelligence inputs due to several operational handicaps .

CCNTS: In 2011, Chhattisgarh started preparations for implementation  of the CCTNS. It invited proposals for system operator for commissioning, operation and maintenance of CCTNS. The state police plans to set up 22 training centers  under the project and the system will cover 398 police stations  across the state.

Goa

UID: The government of Goa entered into an MoU  with UIDAI for the implementation of the UID project through enrollment agencies specifically post offices. The nodal department  for the UID project for the state is the Directorate of Planning and Statistics. The enrollment process was launched  in July 2011 and it was the first state in the country to be covered under the project. It was reported that the UID would be made compulsory for availing any government scheme from November 2012. However, this decision had been criticized  by the opposition and other civil society groups.

NPR: The NPR project was initiated in Goa and enrollment was conducted through data collection camps  which were held in several areas of the state between May-June 2012. The ITI Limited has been given to role of implementing the creation  of the NPR which will cover 14 lakh (1.4 million) people in the state. Information collected as part of the UID project would also be used for the NPR .

CCTV: In March 2012, it was reported that CCTV cameras would be installed in Goa’s beaches  at vital locations which would monitored from a dedicated control room. In September 2012, the government as per the Beach Shack Policy guidelines mandated installation of CCTV cameras in the beach shacks  which would be run round the clock. CCTV camera footage has also been used in the investigation of crimes  in the state. There are several CCTV camera dealers in the state including Lila Digital and Environment Solutions , Armour Office Automation and Drishti Special Response Pvt Ltd.

Drones: No data found.

CCTNS: In 2011, the Goa government started preparations for implementation  of the CCTNS. It invited proposals for system operator for commissioning, operation and maintenance of CCTNS. In March 2013, it was reported that the first phase of the implementation  would be completed by October 2013 and the second phase by March 2014.

Gujarat

UID: The government of Gujarat entered into an MoU  with UIDAI for the implementation of the UID project through enrollment agencies. The nodal department  for the UID project for the state is the General Administration Department. The progress of enrollment  as of May 2013 stands at 1.05 crores (10.5 Million). The Gujarat government also launched  a  separate  UID project specifically for the state. The state project will feature additional information  details like poverty status, income-taxpayer status, permanent address, property ownership and reservation benefits.

NPR: The preparation, survey and enumeration of the NPR was conducted  in Gujarat between April 2010 and February 2011. The population of Gujarat as of 2011 stands at around 6  crores(60 million).

CCTV: CCTV cameras in the state are supplied by the company CCTV Cameras Gujarat . In July 2012, it was reported that a village in the state had installed CCTV cameras around the village and in its primary school.

Drones: In 2011, it was reported that the Indian Navy has deployed a UAV squadorn off the coastal city of Porbandar to detect terror and conventional threats from the sea across the border. As part of Navy's three-tier aerial surveillance grid for the Indian Ocean Region, the drones are used for innermost layer reconnaissance of up to 200 nautical miles.

CCTNS: In 2010, the Gujarat government started preparations for implementation of the CCTNS. 27 district training centers and 4 recruit centers have been established .

Drone: In 2011, it was reported that the Haryana government was planning to procure UAVs  for law enforcement and a senior police officer was of the view that such technologies are useful during rallies and protest marches.

CCTNS: In 2011, the Haryana police started preparations for implementation  of the CCTNS. It invited proposals for system operator for commissioning, operation and maintenance of CCTNS. Madya Pradesh

UID: In March 2010, The UIDAI has entered into an MoU  with the government of Madya Pradesh for the implementation of the UID project through enrollment agencies. Madhya Pradesh became the first Indian state to award a large scale UID-related project  in 2010. The state government inked a Rs 250 crore contract with a consortium of companies headed by the Noida-based HCL Infosystems, part of the HCL group. MP has chosen a slightly different path compared to the pilot projects implemented in places like Chandigarh, which involved the use of smartcards. Instead of handing its citizens electronic storage cards that contain their identities and entitlements, the state has decided to use a combination of hi-tech printing and the Indian postal service to prevent pilferage of subsidized food grains, sugar and fuel.According to the current list, MP has 80 lakh above-the-poverty-line families and 68 lakh below-the-line families, qualifying for subsidies of Rs 125 and Rs 300 per month each respectively. The total subsidy is around Rs 2,500 crore per year, but is expected to decline considerably after the implementation of the scheme.The UID-compliant reconstruction of the PDS database is expected to involve around 7,000-10,000 people and take one year to complete.

NPR: Citizens in Madya Pradesh are being enrolled under the NPR . 

CCTV: CCTV tenders in Madya Pradesh include Vicon Industries  and others.  

Drones: Drones provide a  mechanism for protection and monitoring of wildlife  in the country.  Panna Tiger Reserve in Madhya Pradesh has been selected as a test pilot site for the use of  UAVs.  This hi-tech mechanism will be deployed in other parts of the country as well. This new technology, though in its nascent stage, has been welcomed by the experts. It has been reported that it  will not just help to control poaching but also help in tracking radio collared animals. Further, it may also help in estimation of prey base and other habitat studies more accurately.

CCTNS:  In an attempt  to provide fast, easy and fair police services through the information and technology project in the broader interest of the people. As per the project, all the police stations and administrative offices of the police department will be connected online through a  network, which will cover all1,386 police offices including 956 police stations . TheCCTNS system  aims to digitise the records of the last ten years, and will facilitate collection, storage, retrieval, analysis, transfer and sharing of data and information between the police stations at various levels. Furthermore, people will be able to file a complaint with the state police by using their personal computers from home. For implementing CCTNS in Madya Pradesh, theMadya Pradesh Computerisation of Police Society (MPCOPS)  was formed. The state Mission team is led by the head of State Crime Records Bureau (SCRB).

Manipur

UID: In February 2011 , the UID was officially launched in Manipur through the MoU  with the government of India . In Manipur, the UID enrolment is being undertaken in the hill districts. Through the expression of skepticism of the Central government’s attempt at streamlining delivery of services through Unique Identity Scheme (UID), several privacy advocates have launched anAnti-UID campaign in Manipur .Following instruction from Union ministry of Home Affairs, IGP (Armed Police and Operation) Manipur has asked SPs of all districts to ensure that strict action be taken up against RPF/PLA cadres, who try to disturb the implementation of AADHAR scheme (UID) in Manipur.  

NPR: The NPR started in Manipur  in June 2010. The government of India had announced that the NPR exercise will involve 35 states, 640 districts, 5,767 tehsils, 7,742 towns, 6,08,786 villages, 24 crore households and 1.2 billion population. Over 2.5 million field functionaries will be involved with the exercise. Manipur is the first state which started the collection of biometrics  for the preparation of the NPR along with the Population Enumeration of Census 2011. After the completion of the first phase of head counting in the district, thesecond phase of the National Population Register began in Tamenglong by capturing photographs, two iris images and ten fingers print of all residents who are of age 5 years and above.

CCTV: Spy Action India  supplies CCTV cameras to Manipur.

Drones: No data found.

CCTNS: In 2011 the CCTNS was installed in Manipur. The CCTNS was launched in the state recently soon after the the constitution of State Crime Bureau (SCBR), Manipur under the scheme of NCRB, MHA, Government of India  to work as a clearing house of Crime/Criminal Information by facilitating exchange of information amongst the various state police forces in the country.  An estimated budget of around  Rs 40 to 50 crore is expected for Manipur and an initial amount of Rs 13 crore for the first phase will be given by the Government of India. A system integrator was proposed for the implementation, maintenance and commissioning  of the CCTNS in Manipur. The National Crime Record Bureau NCRB, MHA has already released a total amount of Rs.1,88,96,000  for the implementation of CCTNS in Manipur for maintaining the components for SPMC, capacity building for strengthening of institutional infrastructures, capacity building personnel training in different units of existing districts of the state and setting up of one police training college.
